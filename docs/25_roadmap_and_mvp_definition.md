# 25. Roadmap & MVP Definition

The platform will be developed in three main phases, moving from a niche Polish Bond tool to a comprehensive financial sandbox.

## Phase 1: The Bond Specialist (MVP)
**Goal:** Provide the most accurate Polish Treasury Bond calculator on the web.

### Features:
- **Calculators:** Single bond purchase (EDO, COI, TOS, OTS, ROR, DOR).
- **Domain Logic:** Tax, early redemption, inflation-lag, and capitalization.
- **Data:** Live NBP rates and GUS CPI data ingestion.
- **UX:** Interactive charts (Gross vs Net), detailed breakdown tables, and contextual education.
- **Mobile:** Fully responsive PWA.

## Phase 2: The Strategic Planner (Post-MVP)
**Goal:** Help users manage a collection of investments and plan for goals.

### Features:
- **The Notebook:** Manual entry of bond holdings with maturity tracking.
- **Bond Ladder Simulator:** Modeling recurring monthly purchases.
- **Comparative Simulations:** Side-by-side comparison of different bond issues.
- **Savings Goals:** "House Downpayment" and "Emergency Fund" specific wizards.
- **Export:** PDF reports for saved simulations.

## Phase 3: The Multi-Asset Ecosystem (Vision)
**Goal:** Integrate global assets for a complete wealth-building view.

### Features:
- **New Instruments:** S&P 500, Gold, and Bitcoin.
- **Historical Backtesting:** "What if I invested in 2010?" using real market data.
- **Retirement Planner:** 20-30 year modeling with multi-asset allocation.
- **User Accounts:** Secure, encrypted syncing across devices.
- **Advanced Analytics:** Real Return vs. Inflation for the entire portfolio.

## Future Exploration (Long-term)
- **API for Developers:** Allowing other apps to use our Bond Calculation Engine.
- **Community Scenarios:** Popular templates for "Standard" vs "Aggressive" savings plans.
- **IKE/IKZE Support:** Full integration of tax-advantaged account rules.

---

## MVP Success Criteria
1.  All 6 major Polish retail bond types are accurately modeled.
2.  Inflation data is updated automatically every month.
3.  The UI is intuitive enough for a "Beginner" user (tested via feedback).
4.  Zero floating-point errors in the calculation engine.
