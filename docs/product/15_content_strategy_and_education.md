# 15. Content Strategy & Education

The platform is as much an educational resource as it is a calculator. Our content strategy ensures that every user interaction increases their financial literacy.

## 1. Content Formats

### A. The "What & Why" (Contextual Micro-content)
- Found directly in calculators.
- Explains specific inputs (e.g., "What is a 2-month inflation lag?").
- Uses tooltips and sidebars.

### B. "Deep Dives" (Instrument Guides)
- Dedicated pages for EDO, COI, S&P 500, etc.
- Cover history, mechanics, pros/cons, and tax treatment.
- Use a "Quick Summary" vs "Technical Detail" structure.

### C. The Glossary
- A central repository of financial terms.
- Terms are cross-linked throughout the site.

### D. Scenario Walkthroughs
- "Case studies" using the simulator (e.g., "Saving for a child's education using Family Bonds").
- Shows real-world application of the tools.

## 2. The Learning Ladder
We categorize content by user expertise:
- **Beginner:** "How do I buy my first bond?", "Inflation 101."
- **Intermediate:** "The impact of Belka Tax on long-term compounding," "Bonds vs. Gold."
- **Advanced:** "Modeling real returns in high-inflation environments," "Calculating bond ladder liquidity."

## 3. Neutrality & Ethics
- **No Financial Advice:** We never say "Buy this." We say "This is how this instrument works."
- **Balanced View:** Every instrument guide must include a "Risks & Disadvantages" section.
- **Data Transparency:** We always link to the official prospectus or source data (e.g., GUS).

## 4. Visual Content
- **Animated Charts:** Showing how capitalization "snowballs" over 10 years.
- **Infographics:** Explaining the flow of money from purchase to maturity.
- **Comparison Tables:** For quick feature-by-feature analysis of different bonds.

## 5. Content Maintenance
- Financial rules change (e.g., tax law updates).
- Every article must have a "Last Reviewed" date.
- A central "Legislation Watch" job ensures that if the Belka tax changes, all related content is flagged for update.
