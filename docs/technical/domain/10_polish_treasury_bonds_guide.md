# 10. Polish Treasury Bonds Guide

This guide explains how Polish Treasury bonds work for someone who has
never invested before.

## 1. What Are Polish Treasury Bonds?

Polish Treasury bonds are government debt securities issued by the
Republic of Poland through the Polish Ministry of Finance.

When you buy a bond: - You lend money to the government - The government
pays interest - Your principal is returned at maturity

Example: If you buy a bond for **1,000 PLN**, you will receive interest
and your **1,000 PLN back** when the bond matures.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Why Governments Issue Bonds

Governments issue bonds to finance:

-   Infrastructure
-   Public services
-   Budget deficits
-   Economic programs

Instead of raising taxes immediately, governments borrow from investors.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Why People Buy Polish Bonds

Advantages:

-   Government‑backed (very low risk)
-   Predictable returns
-   Simple to understand
-   Low minimum investment
-   Available online

Many people use them to protect savings from inflation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Types of Polish Retail Bonds

  Bond Type   Duration    Interest Type
  ----------- ----------- -------------------
  3‑month     3 months    Fixed
  1‑year      12 months   Fixed
  2‑year      2 years     Fixed
  3‑year      3 years     Floating
  4‑year      4 years     Inflation indexed
  10‑year     10 years    Inflation indexed

The most popular are **4‑year and 10‑year inflation‑linked bonds**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Important Concepts

### Principal

The amount of money invested.

Example: Principal = 1,000 PLN

### Interest

The payment you receive for lending money.

Example: 1,000 PLN × 5% = 50 PLN per year

### Maturity

The date when the bond ends and your principal is returned.

### Coupon

The interest payment you receive periodically.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Inflation‑Indexed Bonds

Some bonds adjust their interest according to inflation.

Example:

Inflation = 6%\
Bond margin = 1.5%

Total interest = 7.5%

These bonds help protect savings from inflation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. How Buying Polish Bonds Works

### Step 1 --- Decide the Amount

Minimum investment: **100 PLN per bond**

Examples:

  Bonds   Investment
  ------- ------------
  1       100 PLN
  10      1,000 PLN
  100     10,000 PLN

### Step 2 --- Choose Bond Type

Select maturity and interest structure.

### Step 3 --- Purchase

Bonds can be bought:

-   Online through the government bond portal
-   At PKO Bank Polski branches

### Step 4 --- Payment

Money is deducted from your bank account.

Example:

10 bonds × 100 PLN = 1,000 PLN

### Step 5 --- Interest Accumulates

Example:

Investment = 1,000 PLN\
Interest rate = 6%

Yearly interest = 60 PLN

### Step 6 --- Hold the Bond

You simply keep the bond until maturity.

### Step 7 --- Maturity

At maturity you receive:

Principal + final interest.

Example:

Investment: 1,000 PLN\
Total interest: 250 PLN\
Final payout: 1,250 PLN

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Early Redemption

You can withdraw early, but a small fee applies.

Typical fee: **0.70--2 PLN per bond**.

Example:

10 bonds × 2 PLN = 20 PLN fee

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. Taxes

Bond interest is subject to the Polish **capital gains tax (Belka
tax)**.

Tax rate: **19%**

Example:

Interest = 100 PLN\
Tax = 19 PLN\
You receive = 81 PLN

Taxes are usually deducted automatically.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 10. Risks

Even safe bonds have some risks:

-   Inflation risk
-   Liquidity limitations
-   Opportunity cost vs. other investments

## 12. Example Investment

Investment: **5,000 PLN** in 4‑year inflation bonds.

Possible scenario:

  Year   Inflation   Interest
  ------ ----------- --------------------

  2      5%          inflation + margin
  3      4%          inflation + margin

Estimated earnings: **1,000--1,400 PLN** over 4 years.
